I am an AI researcher focused on building adaptive, interpretable systems at the intersection of machine learning, user modeling, and cognitive modeling. While my recent work centers on education, I am broadly committed to human-centered and explainable AI, aiming for accurate, transparent models that support trust in sensitive applications.
I am an incoming assistant professor at the Asian Institute of Technology (AIT) in Thailand. Prior to joining AIT, I worked at AI Research Center, Université du Québec à Montréal, Montreal, Canada, and École Polytechnique, Institut Polytechnique de Paris, French National Centre for Scientific Research (CNRS) and French Institute for Research in Computer Science and Automation (Inria) in France. I received a Ph.D. in Computer Engineering from Université de Montréal in Canada. I also spent time at several international institutions, including Singapore Management University in Singapore, National Institute of Informatics in Japan, and Huaqiao University in China.
